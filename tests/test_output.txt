In my understanding, Microsoft Agent 365 is the enterprise control plane for AI
agents across Microsoft 365. Think of it as the way IT treats agents like      
first-class “digital coworkers” with identity, access, governance, and         
monitoring, so you can scale agent automation without sprawl or risk.          

At a high level, it provides:                                                  

 • Registry and lifecycle: a central inventory to register, track, quarantine, 
   and retire agents.                                                          
 • Identity and access: Entra-based identities and conditional access for      
   agents, with least-privilege policies.                                      
 • Security and compliance: deep integration with Purview (labels/DLP/audit)   
   and Microsoft Defender for threat detection and data safeguards.            
 • Interoperability: works with agents built in Copilot Studio and Azure       
   AI/Foundry, plus popular OSS frameworks, and surfaces in Microsoft 365 apps 
   like Teams, SharePoint, and Dynamics.                                       
 • Observability: analytics and dashboards to see what agents are doing, with  
   anomaly alerts and policy enforcement.                                      

I believe this exists because enterprises need governance that aligns with Zero
Trust and Purview from day one, and Agent 365 fills that gap for multi-agent   
deployments inside Microsoft 365. I’m highly confident on the positioning and  
capabilities described above. For provenance, Microsoft framed Agent 365 during
Ignite 2025 as the control plane for enterprise agents within M365 [Microsoft  
Ignite 2025 Book of News; Microsoft 365 Blog, Nov 2025].
