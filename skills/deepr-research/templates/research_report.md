## Template Usage Guide

This template defines the standard format for presenting Deepr research results.

### Placeholder Descriptions

- `{title}`: Descriptive title derived from research query
- `{summary}`: 2-3 sentence executive summary of findings
- `{findings}`: Bullet list of key discoveries, each with citation
- `{analysis}`: Detailed examination of findings with context
- `{recommendations}`: Actionable next steps based on research
- `{citations}`: Numbered list of sources with URLs
- `{mode}`: Research mode used (quick/standard/deep)
- `{model}`: AI model that performed research
- `{duration}`: Time taken to complete
- `{cost}`: Actual cost incurred
- `{job_id}`: Unique identifier for this research job
- `{timestamp}`: ISO 8601 completion timestamp

### Citation Format

Citations should follow this format:

```
[1] Author/Source. "Title." URL. Accessed {date}.
[2] Organization. "Document Title." URL. Accessed {date}.
```

Inline citations use bracketed numbers: "The market grew 15% [1]."

### Section Guidelines

Summary:
- Maximum 3 sentences
- Lead with most important finding
- Include key metric or conclusion

Key Findings:
- 3-7 bullet points
- Each finding has inline citation
- Ordered by importance

Analysis:
- Expand on findings with context
- Compare/contrast where relevant
- Note limitations or caveats

Recommendations:
- Actionable items only
- Prioritized by impact
- Include effort estimates where possible
